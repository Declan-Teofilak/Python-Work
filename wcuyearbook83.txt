This campus and its buildings 
have survived for 172 years. It is 
a blend of the old and the new. 
Some buildings have been named 
after past contributors to the 
school, others have been named 
for traditions dating back to the 
days of dress codes, curfews, and 
single-sex dorms. This campus 
has grown from a few buildings 
scattered on farmland into a 
thriving college community con- 
sisting of 14 lecture halls on 385 
acres of land. 95 acres make up 
North Campus and 290 make up 
South. One can only wonder how 
all of this will change in another 
172 years.

Life at West Chester used to mean living in a college 
dorm, being in those dorms by 10:00 pm and not being 
allowed to mingle with the opposite sex. Times change 
and rules change. Today students live in many different 
places, with many different lifestyles. Some of us live in 
dorms, some off-campus, and others choose to stay 
home and commute. Some of the places we live in are 
small and neat, others are large and messy; some of us 
live a quite life, others noisy and crowded. No matter 
ho\\ we live or where, we are all members of the com- 
munity of West Chester and will be part of it forever. 
Good memories never fade. And aren't you glad we can 
mingle today? 




PARENT'S DAY 

Parent's Day gave moms and dads the 
chance to see the campus and meet the ad- 
ministration first hand. The day began at 
Sykes where different groups and depart- 
ments had displays. The rainy day and a 
loss to Millersville in football were disap- 
pointments, but spirits were picked up by a 
student talent show and a special dinner 
for parents and students. The highlight of 
the day was being served coffee by Presi- 
dent Dr. Ken Perrin as the guests waited 
for dinner.

OFF CAMPUS 




Frustrated with the limitations 
of dorm life, fed-up with squeal- 
ing freshmen and sick from caf 
food, or maybe the desire for "in- 
dependence", many students 
choose to live "off-campus". The 
neighborhoods near the Univer- 
sity are the homes for hundreds 
of college students. Houses have 
taken on names, Yoder House, 
Football House, Huey House, and 
many have been designated as 
"party houses". Surrounding 
apartment complexes have been 
virtually taken over by students 
— Franklin Court and the off- 
campus party center, Ramsgate. 
Living off campus a student can 
gain independence and skills — 
cooking, cleaning. No more col- 
lect calls home, the phone is in 
your name now. . . independence!

NEW MAIN HALL 




New Main Hall stands today 
where Old Main, a female dorma- 
tory from 1871 to 1971 once stood. 
Within its walls are the Foreign 
Language, Philosophy, English, 
Speech Communication/Theatre 
and History departments. New 
Main auditorium is used weekly 
for the "Free Wednesday Night 
Movie" and daily for classes, spe- 
cial lectures and seminars. 



LRC 




The Learning Resource Center, 
on the corner of Rosedale and 
High Streets, houses a variety of 
different services offered to West 
Chester students. It is frequented 
by students using the computers, 
by students and visitors to see 
performances in the Turk's Head 
Theatre, and by the hundreds of 
students each semester to see the 
registrar and accounts payable 
offices. The lines for drop/add 
are known to run the length of the 
building, both inside and out. 
And, of course, anxious seniors 
flow, fingers crossed, to the 
graduation office.

SWOPE 



The halls of this academic

auditorium where performances

This year Swope was also the 
stage for a number of outside 
greats, such as Buddy Rich and 
violinist Frances Fortier.

CHAMBER CHOIR 



The West Chester University Chamber Choir Is directed by Jane 
Sheppard, Associate Professor In the Vocal Choral Department of the 
School of Music. The Chamber Choir consists of 25 members who are 
chosen by audition. They specialize In singing music of the Renais- 
sance and Baroque periods. In December of '83 the Choir produced Its

began here at West Chester 
University in September 
1974. It was founded by a 
group of returning women 
students. The purpose of the 
Women's Center is to serve 
the special needs of the 
woman student whether she 
is a nontraditional or tradi- 
tional student.

students. The faculty provides services for a wide range of concerns 
which may include, helping a student to clarify vocational or education- 
al choices, improve interpersonal skills or resolve personal conflicts.
